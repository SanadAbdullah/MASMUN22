Historical War Council

The Historical War Council is an inimitable committee as it is a war simulator that focuses on keeping up universal peace and security, forming alliances amongst countries, and, most importantly, casting a light on the value of harmonic negotiation amongst countries for the sake of fulfilling common ends. Not like any other committee, the Historical War Council requires Generals, instead of delegates, who are supposed to invade countries, form alliances, write battle plans, and accomplish specific goals, all for the utter aim of ensuring the best outcome for both their people and their allied nations.

Topic 1

World War 1

“This is a war to end all wars.”— Woodrow Wilson, 1917

World War I, often known as the Great War, began in 1914 after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria. His assassination triggered a European war that lasted until 1918. Germany, Austria-Hungary, Bulgaria, and the Ottoman Empire (the Central Powers) battled against the United Kingdom, France, Russia, Italy, Romania, Japan, and the United States during the war (the Allied Powers). World War I saw unparalleled levels of bloodshed and destruction due to new military technology and the horrors of trench warfare. By the time the war ended and the Allies declared victory, more than 16 million people had died, both soldiers and civilians.
