As a court of last resort, it seeks to complement, not replace the national courts. Topic 1

The Prosecutor v. Ali Muhammad Ali Abd-Al-Rahman ("Ali Kushayb")

Ali Muhammad Ali Abd-Al-Rahman, also known as Ali Kushayb, is a prominent Janjaweed leader who fought alongside the Sudanese government against Darfur rebel groups under the administration of President Omar al-Bashir

Ali Muhammad Ali Abd-Al-Rahman was transferred to the ICC's custody on 9 June 2020, after surrendering himself voluntarily in the Central African Republic. The initial appearance of Mr Abd-Al-Rahman before the ICC took place on 15 June 2020. According to the Prosecution's submission of the Document Containing the Charges, Mr Abd-Al-Rahman is suspected of 31 counts of war crimes and crimes against humanity allegedly committed between August 2003 and at least April 2004 in Darfur, Sudan. The confirmation of charges hearing took place from 24 to 26 May 2021.

Topic 2

The Prosecutor v. Dominic Ongwen

Dominic Ongwen, a former child soldier who rose to become one of the rebel Lord's Resistance Army's (LRA) senior leaders, was sentenced to 25 years in jail for war crimes and crimes against humanity in Uganda by the International Criminal Court (ICC). The 45-year-old was convicted guilty in February 2021 of 61 crimes, including killings, rapes, and sexual slavery, committed during the LRA's reign of terror in the early 2000s, commanded by the fugitive Joseph Kony.

The Lord's Resistance Army has been one of central Africa's cruelest and most enduring armed groups over the past 30 years. The LRA has abducted over 67,000 youth, including 30,000 children, for use as child soldiers, sex slaves, and porters, and has brutalized communities since its inception in 1987.

Topic 3

The Prosecutor v. Stalin

Joseph Stalin was the Communist Party of the Soviet Union's secretary-general and premier of the Soviet state for a quarter-century, dictatorially ruling the Soviet Union and transforming it into an international power. Stalin industrialized the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics, forced the modernization of agriculture, and used police brutality to solidify his power. Furthermore, he continuously purged the Communist party, for Ninety three of the 139 Central Committee members were killed and 81 of the 103 generals and admirals were executed. Also, three million people were accused of opposing Communism, and they were sent to the gulag, a system of labor camps in Siberia, all under the control of Joseph Stalin.
