Urgency for Justice

      For as long as humans have existed, a need for justice has coursed through our veins. From Socrates’s execution, to the controversy of the United States’ amendment to bear arms, the justice system has repeatedly failed its people with its lack of proper regulation. It is proven that, to this day, justice is not served.


      It is with recent traction that these issues have been brought to the limelight. For one, the Black Lives Matter movement is one that continues to fight for racial equality, an issue that should have been resolved with the amendments of the late 19th century. Moreover, the cruelty of honour killings in the Middle Eastern region has also gained social traction through public posts and coverage on social media. The latter additionally adds fuel to the uproar of the third wave of feminism; women fight for their right to be treated equally on a daily basis.


It is seen that the fight for justice is historical, relevant, and global, regardless of the specific situation.


      Abide all, the fight for justice is still a war composed of many battles to come. The road to equality is an unpaved road leading to the unexplored territory of peak humanity. Above all, alarms will continue to siren with the need and the urgency to achieve justice for all.


The urgency for justice is one that will never be silenced.
